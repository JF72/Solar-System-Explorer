Mercury

Mercury, the closest planet to the sun, is not the hottest planet in our solar system despite being only 36 million miles away. It has desert-like temperature extremes with scorching days and freezing nights.

Go to Mercury
Venus

Venus, the second closest planet to the sun at 67 million miles away, is the hottest planet in the solar system. It's extreme heat is caused by its extremely thick atmosphere that traps all the heat within it.

Go to Venus
Earth

Earth, the third planet from the sun, is the only known planet to support life, with a protective atmosphere that shields us from harmful radiation and moderate temperature ranges suitable for diverse ecosystems.

Go to Earth
Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from the sun, is known as the "Red Planet" due to its rusty appearance and has a thin atmosphere with frigid temperature ranges that vary between day and night.

Go to Mars

Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet in the solar system, has a thick atmosphere with extreme wind speeds and temperature variations that occur between its poles and equator.

Go to Jupiter
Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the sun, is known for its iconic rings made of ice and rock particles, with atmospheric temperature ranges and extreme wind speeds.

Go to Saturn
Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet from the sun, has a unique tilt that causes it to appear to roll on its side, with extreme seasonal temperature changes and a thin ring system.

Go to Uranus
Neptune

Neptune, the eighth planet from the sun, has a blue hue due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere and extreme wind speeds that cause storms and temperature variations.
